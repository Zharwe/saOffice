State's Attorney's Office

The State's Attorney is the Chief Law Enforcement Officer for Macon County, and is the Chief Legal Adviser for the county. The office is an agency of the Executive Branch of state government. In addition to the elected State's Attorney, the Office is currently comprised of 16 Assistant State's Attorneys, 2 Investigators, and 14 full-time support staff personnel.

The Macon County State's Attorney's office is broken down into the following divisions, units and programs:

Divisions
  • Felony Division
  • Civil Division
  • Misdemeanor & Juvenile Division
  • DUI & Traffic Division
Units
  • Elderly Victims Crime Unit
  • Gang and Weapons Prosecution Unit
  • Sex Crimes Unit
  • Drug Prosecution Unit
  • Domestic Violence Unit
  • Investigations Unit
  • Victim Witness Unit
Programs
  • Adult Diversion Program
  • Deferred Prosecution Program
  • Community Outreach Program
  • Internships Program
